DreamSpace

Gravity, Resistance, and Control

Game Concept

DreamSpace evolves from its original form where users could freely move through space and travel between different planets using portals. You can view the original Dream Space version here. This version introduces a new layer: goals and constraints through gravitational forces and item collection.

A black hole continuously pulls everything inward, including the player. While being drawn toward it, the player must complete specific quests rather than collecting random items. For example, in the quest “Eat the hamburger set,” the player needs to collect a hamburger, fries, cola, and ketchup to progress to the next stage.

The game focuses on the tension between being pulled and resisting that force, creating a fundamental conflict that shapes every moment of gameplay.

Conceptual Framing

This game explores how systems respond to physical input and change the player's experience.

Core Goal

  • Identify the current quest objective for the stage
  • Collect all the specific food items required by that quest within 2 minutes
  • Reach the black hole after completing the full set

Game Mechanics

Quest System

  • Each stage gives the player a specific collection objective
  • Only the required food items count toward progression
  • Completing the quest unlocks access to the next stage

Black Hole System

  • All objects are constantly pulled toward the black hole
  • The player cannot escape this force completely
  • Movement is always influenced by gravity while pursuing quest items

Control System

  • Joystick → Left/right and up/down movement
  • Button → Collect item
  • Pressure sensor → Resist and push away from the black hole's gravitational pull

Input Mapping

  • Joystick adjusts the player's horizontal and vertical position
  • Pressing the button collects a quest item on contact
  • Pressure sensor input controls the force to resist and push away from the black hole

Control System

The first controller used two potentiometers to control left/right and up/down movement. However, it was too difficult to operate precisely, so the input was replaced with a joystick for smoother and more intuitive control. (Much better!!)

Process & Development

Reference images of objects were collected and converted into 3D models to be used as in-game items.

NPC and collectible items were placed throughout the environment.

A quest UI was implemented to guide the player.

This script controls player movement, combining Arduino input and keyboard controls to simulate zero-gravity navigation, item collection, and interaction with a black hole force system.

Score, timer, and position indicators were added to the interface. Each item gives 10 points, and the game has a 2-minute time limit. A fail screen with sound appears automatically when time runs out. Position indicators were used to help track NPCs in a zero-gravity environment.

Once all items are collected, the player can enter the portal.

The player arrives on another planet through the portal.
