DreamSpace

Gravity, Resistance, and Control

Game Concept

DreamSpace evolves from its original form where users could freely move through space and travel between different planets using portals. You can view the original Dream Space version here. This version introduces a new layer: goals and constraints through gravitational forces and item collection.

A black hole continuously pulls everything inward, including the player. While being drawn toward it, the player must complete specific quests rather than collecting random items. For example, in the quest “Eat the hamburger set,” the player needs to collect a hamburger, fries, cola, and ketchup to progress to the next stage.

The game focuses on the tension between being pulled and resisting that force, creating a fundamental conflict that shapes every moment of gameplay.

Core Goal

  • Identify the current quest objective for the stage
  • Collect the specific food items required by that quest
  • Reach the black hole after completing the full set

Game Mechanics

Quest System

  • Each stage gives the player a specific collection objective
  • Only the required food items count toward progression
  • Completing the quest unlocks access to the next stage

Black Hole System

  • All objects are constantly pulled toward the black hole
  • The player cannot escape this force completely
  • Movement is always influenced by gravity while pursuing quest items

Control System

  • Potentiometer 1 → left and right movement
  • Potentiometer 2 → up and down movement
  • Button → collect item
  • Pressure sensor → control force toward or away from gravity

Input Mapping

  • Potentiometer 1 adjusts the player's horizontal position
  • Potentiometer 2 adjusts the player's vertical position
  • Pressing the button collects a quest item on contact
  • Pressure sensor input changes the force of moving closer to or farther from the black hole

Core Experience

The player does not feel fully in control. Instead, they experience constant tension between being pulled and resisting.

“I am being pulled.”
“I have to resist.”
“I need to apply force.”

Conceptual Framing

This game explores how systems respond to physical input and change the player's experience.
