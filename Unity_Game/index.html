DreamSpace

Gravity, Resistance, and Perceptual Control

Game Concept

DreamSpace evolves from its original form where users could freely move through space and travel between different planets using portals. This version introduces a new layer: goals and constraints through gravitational forces and item collection.

A black hole continuously pulls everything inward, including the player. While being drawn toward it, the player must collect floating items and try to reach the black hole with a sufficient score. The game focuses on the tension between being pulled and resisting that force—creating a fundamental conflict that shapes every moment of gameplay.

Core Goal

  • Collect as many floating food items as possible
  • Reach the black hole with a sufficient score
  • Pass through the black hole to the next level
  • If the score is too low → restart

Game Mechanics

Black Hole System

  • All objects are constantly pulled toward the black hole
  • The player cannot escape this force completely
  • Movement is always influenced by gravity

Control System

  • Joystick → direction
  • Pressure (stress ball + FSR sensor) → speed / force

Input Mapping

  • Weak pressure → fast movement
  • Strong pressure → slow movement
  • No pressure → pulled directly into black hole

Core Experience

The player does not feel fully in control. Instead, they experience constant tension between being pulled and resisting.

“I am being pulled.”
“I have to resist.”
“I need to apply force.”

Conceptual Framing

This game explores how systems interpret physical input and reshape perception. The player does not directly control movement—instead, their physical force is translated into system behavior.

This creates a gap between intention and outcome, revealing how computational systems are not neutral, but actively structure experience.
